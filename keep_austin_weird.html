Keep Austin Weird

Why is this the city slogan? We think it’s because of the many festivals, places, artists, and musicians that are special to Austin. Its community supports originality and local-born businesses, like no other.

Below we listed some Austin gems that we think you’ll enjoy, including a few tips for Cinco de Mayo (for those who’ll be around the Monday after the wedding). The city has lots more to offer than what’s listed below; we focused on places that are special to us.

Austin is the city where we met and fell in love — we’re excited to share it with you on our wedding weekend!

Downtown

24 Diner, Restaurant
We once ran into Reggie Watts here.

Alamo Drafthouse Ritz, Movie Theater
Many theaters serve good grub and beer, but there’s nothing like the Alamo. Staff-picked previews (vintage film clips, music videos, etc.) set the scene before your movie and make it a special experience. Once, Leonard Nimoy surprised an Alamo audience expecting to see The Wrath of Khan, and he announced that they would see the world premiere of the new Star Trek film, instead. ‘Nuff said.

Grant says: See if they have any events like old movies with special guests, or Master Pancake Theater (think MST3K).

Vanessa says: Try the chocolate chip cookies! Mmmm-azing.

Amy’s Ice Creams (One of many locations), Desserts

The Best Wurst, Food Truck

La Condesa Cinco De Mayo Block Party, Event
We are pretty sure this is an annual event. Also, every year, we think there is a celebration at Fiesta Gardens, plus Mexicarte presents “Cinco de Mayo: La Battalla” at the Paramount Theater. Closer to the trip, we recommend checking out Do512.com for fun Cinco de Mayo events!

Congress Bridge/Austin Bats, Sightseeing
The Congress Avenue bats reside here as early as March, but the best bat views are in August when the young pups join their mothers in nightly flights. This May, you might just catch a few of them hanging around ;)

The Driskill, Bar/Lounge
This gorgeous, historic hotel is said to be haunted! Might be worth looking into ghost tours. Or, ditch Casper to have a swanky cocktail at The Driskill Bar.

Easy Tiger, Bar/Lounge
Laid back, nice atmosphere, good food. These days, it’s our favorite spot downtown.

East Sixth Street, Bar/Lounges
East Sixth Street is technically “East Austin,” not Downtown. East of I-35 there are a variety of great bars, ranging from laid-back to posh. It has a different crowd than the famed Sixth Street, which is frequented more by tourists.

Vanessa says: Rio Rita is where many of us grad students would hang out during my UT days. It’s known for its Bloody Mary.

Franklin Barbecue, Food Truck
We always hear amazing things about this barbecue, but we’ve never been able to try it! They usually have long lines and are only open for lunch (from 11am until they sell out).

Halcyon, Café/Desserts
This swanky coffee shop is known for their tableside s’mores.

Lustre Pearl, Bar/Lounge

MexiCarte Museum, Art/Museums

The Paramount, Theater
We bumped into Weird Al Yankovic here during a screening of UHF. We still have a spatula from that night.

The Roaring Fork, Restaurant

Waterloo Records, Music Shop/Historic

Whole Foods, Grocery Store
This location is the flagship store and currently the company headquarters. Whole Foods began in Austin!

South Congress (SoCo)

Allen’s Boots, Shopping

Hey Cupcake! South Austin (One of many locations), Desserts

Hotel San Jose, Bar/Lounge
This hotel has a stunning, string-lit, backyard lounge with famously delicious cocktails.

Vanessa says: The Champassion is lovely.

Grant says: They have the best Michelada you will ever try.

Trail at Lady Bird Lake: Auditorium Shores, Parks & Recreation

Magnolia Café South (One of two locations), Restaurant

Vanessa says: The mudd is the best thing on their menu. But their peanut butter chocolate pie might make you fall in love – literally. I raved about this pie to Grant when we began dating. One day, during a particularly busy semester at UT, Grant asked me to take the night off to go out with him. I declined, feeling obliged to stay in and catch up with work. That evening, he surprised me at home. I will never forget the sweet moment I opened my door and found him standing there with that brown paper take-out bag from Magnolia. I loved this persistent, pie-bearing man.

New Bohemia & all the other vintage shops on the SoCo strip!

Thundercloud Subs Town Lake (One of many locations), Fast Food
Simple and delicious Austin-based sandwich joint. We love the “Office Favorite.”

South Austin (West of SoCo)

Barton Springs Pool & Zilker Park, Parks & Recreation

Rudy’s, Restaurant
Not to be confused with Ruby’s, this chain restaurant has many locations and serves amazing St. Louis ribs.

Torchy’s Tacos South (One of many locations), Food Truck/Tex Mex

East Austin

Live Oak Brewery Tour, Beer
Wonderful, local brewery known for their Hefeweisen.

Okay Mountain, Art Gallery

The Serie Project, Affordable Art Prints
Art for everyone – browse their flat files and you are sure to find a print you’ll love. Originals too, artist signed ;)

El Taco Rico, Taco Truck
Right next to the Serie Project on Felix Ave. and Vargas Rd. Thinking about all that conceptual art will make you work up an appetite. Try the barbacoa taco – soft, cheeky, meaty, mmm.

University Area & Central Austin

Arcade UFO, Fun/Games

The Blanton Museum of Art, Art Museum
This amazing museum is located at The University of Texas. Come visit an exhibit as well as Vanessa’s alma matter!

The Contemporary Laguna Gloria, Art Museum

The Daniel Johnston Frog Mural, Public Art/Sightseeing
Guadalupe & 21st Street.

The Draught House, Pub
They always have several home-brews on tap.

The Flying Saucer, Pub

Mayfield Park & Mount Bonnell, Parks & Recreation
Visit this park to hang out with some peacocks.

Ruby’s BBQ, Restaurant

Tacodeli Rosedale (One of three locations), Restaurant

Grant says: Yes, the name sounds weird. No, it’s not a deli. Hands down the best tacos anywhere. I’ve eaten the entire menu. You can’t go wrong. I like the puerco borracho, the mole chicken, the ceviche, or my favorite: El Picosito (Mondays only).

The Steeping Room at North Lamar (One of two locations), Tea/Restaurant

Texas French Bread, Restaurant

Vanessa says: The Weekend Warrior = Pimento cheese ½ sandwich + cup o’ chicken tortilla soup + snickerdoodle cookie. Best lunch combo EVER.

Vino Vino, Wine Bar/Restaurant
We had our first date here!

Outside the Austin City Limits

Kreuz Market, Restaurant
To try this dry rub (no sauce) barbecue, you'd have to drive out to Lockhart, TX. It’s our favorite barbecue place ever -- we think it’s worth the drive.

The Salt Lick, Restaurant
This famous barbecue joint is located near our wedding venue in Dripping Springs/Driftwood. It is absolutely delicious and lives up to its reputation!
